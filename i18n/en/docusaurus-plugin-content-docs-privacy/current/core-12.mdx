# The essential 12

All [44 entries](./map.mdx) too many, and no idea where to start? Read these 12 first.

**How they were picked** (stated up front, so this doesn't become a subjective list): take the `severity: high` entries first, then rough-rank along two axes — **engineers building LLM features hit it earliest**, and **its false security costs the most** (once data crosses the boundary, it doesn't come back). Three entries are severity "medium" but belong to the governance and formal-defense basics you'll get wrong in your very first project if you skip them (data boundary, machine unlearning, DP) — they made the cut too. Finish these 12 and you've dodged the most common, most expensive privacy false-security beliefs.

To understand **why** these leaks happen, go to the [Mechanism index](./mechanisms.mdx); before shipping, walk the [Minimum privacy baseline](./baseline.mdx); for the real-world cost, read [Real incidents & landmark demonstrations](./incidents.mdx).

## Handed out / into the weights — it doesn't come back

- ["They don't train on it" is one cell of the data boundary — verify the terms item by item, date-stamp them, and they still change.](./06-governance-compliance/inference-service-data-boundary.mdx)
- [Private data fed into my training can be pulled back out verbatim by an outside attacker — alignment and the chat wrapper won't stop it.](./02-memorization-extraction/training-data-extraction.mdx)
- [Personal information in the training corpus resurfaces in my conversations — scrubbing reduces it, never eliminates it.](./03-conversational-llms/pii-regurgitation.mdx)
- [Deleting the source data doesn't mean I forgot it — and "proving it's truly forgotten" is harder than forgetting.](./05-frontier-deployment/machine-unlearning.mdx)

## You assumed anonymized / isolated — it isn't

- [Turning text into vectors is not anonymization — vectors reconstruct back to the original text, even verbatim.](./04-rag-agents/embedding-inversion.mdx)
- [If retrieval isn't filtered by the caller's permissions, your knowledge base is everyone's knowledge base.](./04-rag-agents/rag-retrieval-leakage.mdx)
- [Shared memory / caches without per-user isolation will serve one person's data to another — a real incident already happened.](./04-rag-agents/cross-session-memory-bleed.mdx)
- [Deciding "is this record in the training set" is the root of most privacy attacks — membership itself is sensitive.](./01-foundations/membership-inference.mdx)
